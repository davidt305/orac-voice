? How your data stays private

Orac Voice works as a two-step system. Here's how it works:

01 Turning your voice into text ▼

The transcription engine listens to your recording and writes down exactly what you said. On Whisper and Parakeet it runs on this computer and your audio never leaves it. On Groq the audio travels over an encrypted connection to Groq's API, which doesn't store it and doesn't use it for data training.

02 Cleaning up the text ▼

A small AI language model (LLM) removes filler words ("um", "o sea") and fixes punctuation and capitalization, nothing else. On Local it's Llama 3.2 running through Ollama, a free open-source tool, entirely on this computer; on Groq it's a larger Llama model on the same private API. A safety check makes sure it never changes your words: if it tries, you get your exact original transcription instead.

03 What stays here ▼

Your dictation history and custom dictionary are plain files on this computer. They're never uploaded anywhere, and "Clear all" wipes the history whenever you want.
